What is Home Loan EMI?

Home Loan EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed monthly payment made by a borrower to repay a home loan. It consists of both interest and principal components.

Home Loan EMI Formula

EMI = P × R × (1 + R)N / [(1 + R)N – 1]

  • P = Home Loan Amount
  • R = Monthly Interest Rate
  • N = Number of Monthly Installments

Example of Home Loan EMI Calculation

If you take a home loan of ₹50,00,000 at an interest rate of 8.5% per annum for 20 years, your monthly EMI will be approximately ₹43,391.

Factors Affecting Home Loan EMI

  • Loan amount
  • Interest rate
  • Loan tenure
  • Type of interest rate (fixed or floating)

Understanding Home Loan Amortization

In a home loan, the interest portion is higher in the initial years. As the loan progresses, the principal repayment increases, reducing the outstanding balance gradually.

FAQs

Is home loan EMI fixed?

For fixed-rate loans, EMI remains the same. For floating-rate loans, EMI may change.

Can prepayment reduce home loan interest?

Yes, prepaying your home loan reduces the outstanding principal and total interest payable.
