What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount paid by a borrower every month to repay a loan. It includes both the principal amount and the interest charged by the lender.

EMI Calculation Formula

The EMI is calculated using the following formula:

EMI = P × R × (1 + R)N / [(1 + R)N – 1]

  • P = Loan Amount
  • R = Monthly Interest Rate
  • N = Number of Monthly Installments

Example of EMI Calculation

If you take a loan of ₹5,00,000 at an interest rate of 10% per annum for a tenure of 5 years, your monthly EMI will be approximately ₹10,624.

Factors Affecting EMI

  • Loan amount
  • Interest rate
  • Loan tenure
  • Type of loan

Understanding the Amortization Schedule

The amortization schedule shows how each EMI payment is divided into interest and principal. Initially, the interest portion is higher, and over time the principal repayment increases.

FAQs

Is EMI the same every month?

Yes, for fixed-rate loans, the EMI remains the same throughout the loan tenure.

Can I reduce my EMI?

You can reduce EMI by increasing the loan tenure or negotiating a lower interest rate.

Does prepayment reduce interest?

Yes, prepayment reduces the outstanding principal, which lowers the total interest payable.
