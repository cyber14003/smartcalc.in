What is SIP?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds. It helps investors build wealth over time through disciplined investing.

SIP Calculation Formula

The SIP maturity amount is calculated using the formula:

M = P × [(1 + r)n − 1] / r × (1 + r)

  • P = Monthly investment amount
  • r = Monthly rate of return
  • n = Number of months

Example of SIP Calculation

If you invest ₹5,000 per month for 10 years at an expected return of 12% per annum, your total investment will be ₹6,00,000 and the estimated value will be around ₹11.6 lakhs.

Benefits of SIP

  • Disciplined investing
  • Power of compounding
  • Rupee cost averaging
  • Flexible investment amount

FAQs

Is SIP better than lump sum?

SIP reduces market timing risk and is suitable for long-term investors.

Can I stop SIP anytime?

Yes, SIPs can be stopped or modified at any time without penalties.
